My Vision of India

When someone asks me, “What is your vision of India?” my first thought is usually about food. Imagine an India where samosas are tax-free, pani puri never finishes, and every home has a fridge full of mangoes! Okay, jokes apart, my real vision of India is something bigger and brighter.

I dream of an India where no student says, “I can’t study because of poor internet.” My vision is that every corner, from Ladakh to Kanyakumari, has strong Wi-Fi. (Let’s be honest, if we can survive without food for a day, maybe we can—but without Wi-Fi? Impossible!)

I want to see an India where politicians argue less, and maybe play cricket to decide who wins elections. At least then we’ll all enjoy watching! But on a serious note, I wish for leaders who work together to solve problems rather than create new ones.

In my India, education will not be a headache but a joy. No more heavy school bags that feel like we’re training for the Olympics weightlifting event. Instead, smart classrooms, creative teaching, and yes—homework that doesn’t take three hours of our life every day!

Another part of my vision is about equality. I imagine an India where no one is judged by their caste, color, or gender, but only by their character. Where a boy who wants to dance is not laughed at, and a girl who wants to be a scientist is not stopped.

And of course, jobs! My vision is an India where every youth finds work not just for earning money, but also for passion. An India where start-ups bloom like spring flowers, and every young person feels proud to say, “I’m making a difference.”

Finally, my biggest vision is unity. Imagine 1.4 billion people, all different, but standing together like one big cricket team. If we can cheer for India during World Cup matches, then why not cheer for India every single day?

So yes, my vision of India is not just about tall buildings or fast trains. It’s about happy people, fair opportunities, and a nation that is both modern and kind.

That is my dream—and I believe if we youth work for it, this dream will become reality.
