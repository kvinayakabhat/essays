

Good morning respected teachers, judges, and my dear friends,

Today, I am here to talk about something that has taken over our lives so quietly that we didn’t even notice it—Artificial Intelligence, or AI.

Now, before you think AI is something complicated, let me ask—how many of you have secretly used ChatGPT to write essays or homework? Don’t worry, I won’t tell your teacher! Even I once asked AI to write a poem for my best friend’s birthday, and trust me, the poem was so sweet that even my mother thought I wrote it!

AI is literally everywhere. Can’t fix a laptop? Ask AI. Don’t know why your bike is making a weird sound? AI might just tell you the problem. Bored? Ask AI to generate an image of you as a Bollywood hero, a superhero, or even as a king riding a dinosaur! (Warning: you may look cooler in AI than in real life!)

But here’s the catch. While AI can do everything, it also makes us… a little too lazy. Why learn painting if AI can make Mona Lisa in your selfie version? Why practice essays if ChatGPT can give you one instantly? And the biggest problem—if machines start doing everything, humans may lose jobs. Imagine robots cooking food, driving buses, or even teaching math! One day we might say, “Alexa, please do my homework,” and Alexa replies, “No, do it yourself!”

Still, AI is not a monster. It is like a super-smart helper, but only if we control it. We humans have something AI doesn’t—feelings, creativity, and the ability to laugh at silly jokes. That’s why instead of fearing AI, we should learn to use it wisely.

In the end, I believe AI can be our best friend, but only if we remain the boss. So let’s use AI to make life easier, not to make humans disappear.

Thank you!
